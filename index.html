Brief History

Madrasah is a community-based institution that provides education in the Arabic language and Islam. The institution was created through the President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo issue of the Executive Order No. 283. This was an order to create a committee to facilitate the Madrasah development.

The office was created to be the center of the basic governments services in muslim communities all over Davao City. But the office admits how the Madrasah development is being hindered due to a lack of resources but the system has potential.

The Mission

The organizations exist because they aim to prepare Muslim children to become “assiduous, committed Muslims” in pursuing endeavors that are beneficial to themselves. They teach the children about their history, about heir values, teachings of Islam, writing in Arabic, and many more.

The institution promotes a lot of things such as promoting EID or Islamic Festivals and developing unique feats of various Muslim tribes in Davao City and Promote Davao City as a peaceful city where all cultures and religions harmoniously coexist. The beneficiaries that are being given by the service are Muslim children, who wants to learn about their culture and religion.

The institution also aims to help the Muslim children especially when the pandemic hit through providing them daily supplies.

The Organizers

The organizers of this institution are the MCDPU, they aim to educate Muslim children with the “true values”, history, Arabic writing and reading, science and teachings of Islam to become a law-abiding citizen contributing to social and moral development.

Community reach-out programs promoting Eid or Islamic Festivals and developing unique feat of various Muslim tribes in Davao City. Document assistance to the Muslim constituents in Davao City. Promote Davao City as a peaceful city where all cultures and religions harmoniously coexist.

Beneficiaries and Benefactors

As a result of this program, Muslim children get to feel closer to their culture and religion, and be able to participate in many cultural festivities. They give the children who lack education the knowledge of writing and reading their own Arabic language, so that they may be able to thrive when they grow. Parents can get to see their child learn their ways and be able to include them in any religious activities, as well as provide them with what they need. And other people outside the organization too can know about the hardships of Muslims and how they can help.

AusAID funds Dep-Ed to this day. This is because the Australian government handed over 500 million dollars (27,692,000,000 Php) to 1,500 Islamic schools to gain national accreditation.
